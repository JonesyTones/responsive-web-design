            <li><strong>1962:</strong> Marley forms a musical group called The Wailers with Peter Tosh and Bunny Livingston (later known as Bunny Wailer).</li>
            <li><strong>1972:</strong> The Wailers sign a record deal with Island Records and release their breakthrough album, "Catch a Fire."</li>
            <li><strong>1973:</strong> Marley and The Wailers release their iconic album, "Burnin'," featuring the hit song "I Shot the Sheriff."</li>
            <li><strong>1976:</strong> Marley survives an assassination attempt just days before the "Smile Jamaica" concert, which he still performs at despite being injured.</li>
            <li><strong>1977:</strong> Bob Marley is diagnosed with malignant melanoma, a type of skin cancer.</li>
            <li><strong>1978:</strong> Marley and The Wailers perform at the "One Love Peace Concert" in Jamaica, where he famously brings rival politicians Michael Manley and Edward Seaga on stage, attempting to ease political tensions.</li>
            <li><strong>1979:</strong> Marley releases the album "Survival," which focuses on political and social issues in Jamaica and Africa.</li>
            <li><strong>1980:</strong> Marley performs his last concert in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, before his health deteriorates.</li>
            <li><strong>1981:</strong> Bob Marley passes away on May 11 at the age of 36 due to complications from cancer.</li>
